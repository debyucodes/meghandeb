The Rest of our lives

Issue #1

In the year of our reckoning, amidst the long shadow of the Great Plague (known in lesser tongues as COVID-19), two souls set forth on a most unexpected adventure.

Separated by lands yet connected by the magic of the ether (or, as the wise call it, FaceTime), Meg and Deb first beheld each other’s countenances through the shimmering light of a screen. Their first nights were spent in merriment, watching grand performances of dazzling queens in a virtual Drag Concert Hall, their laughter echoing through the unseen threads of fate.

I did my waiting! Four months of it!

But the time came when mere visions and echoes would no longer suffice. With great courage (and six feet of distance), they embarked upon their first quest into the wilds—an encounter where no hands were clasped, yet hearts drew near. They traversed the perils of masked greetings and frostbitten outdoor walks, forging a bond stronger than mithril. Through victories and trials, their fellowship grew—two became one, and with them, their noble companions of fur and paw joined in harmonious kinship.

Happiness can be found, even in the darkest of times, if one only remembers to turn on the light

But no great tale is without sorrow. In the darkness, they faced sickness, loss, and the aching weight of farewells. Yet, even in the deepest night, they found light in one another—offering solace, love, and a steadfast promise to walk together, come what may.

Now, as they stand upon the threshold of a new chapter, a great feast is called, and banners are raised! Friends, kin, and kindred spirits shall gather to witness the forging of a new alliance, a bond sealed in love and laughter.

The Proposal

Through the Highlands fair, they journey forth,
Where Glen Coe’s peaks did rise,
By Hagrid’s hut and shimmering pond,
‘Neath the ever-changing sky.

Deb led Meg down a tricky path,
Near slips, but no tumble came,
As the rain began to pour,
A picture snapped, Deb tongue tied.

Then down on one knee,
Deb asked with tears in her eye,
“Meghan Corry,
Will you marry me?”

The moment stretched like enchanted mist,
Time seemed to hold its breath,
“Baby, it’s on the ground,” she said
For the ring had slipped and tumbled down.
